After the whirlwind that was Venice, I was relieved to be traveling in the company of my NYU New York roommate, still not at ease with solo-

traveling. I was so determined I did not mind booking a 6:00 AM flight. Somnolent yet eager I did not sleep on the flight.

Since we both planned on studying abroad during the fall semester of 2025 we had agreed to take trips together. She'd fly in from London and I from Paris. Mallorca was one of the destinations we had both agreed we were most excited for.

It was a place where we would be able to soak up the sun. Low and behold Mallorca did not let us down. It exceeded our expectations, it gave us not only the crystaline waters, but amazing views of the island's mountains and a charming city center. Our stay was near the airport which meant we had to take public transit into the city center anytime we wanted to go anywhere as the bus lines were very limited. Determined to explore as many coastal villages as we could we thrust ourselves into the unknown.

Mallorca was unreal, everywhere we turned we found ourselves in admiration. We both contemplated how fortunate the island's natives must be to be able to be surrounded by it daily. During the four days we spent there we took advantage of the sea while basking in the sun, unintentionally hiked to some rewarding views, and savored delicious food and stunning architecture in the city center.

Looking back on the trips I've taken during my time abroad Mallorca stands firm as my favorite destination. It reminded me of the long, forgotten summers of childhood, where the only goal was to feel the sun on your skin and discover what lay around the next bend. I was a child again, captivated by the simple magic of a sunbeam.
